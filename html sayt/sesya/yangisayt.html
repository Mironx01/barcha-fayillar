U.S. travel to Cuba is expected to rebound in
2019, according to a new survey released by
the Center for Responsible Travel (CREST). The
independent survey of U.S. tour operators and
other service providers to Cuba shows that
people-to-people travel, study abroad programs
and cruise bookings are expected to rise this
year after seeing declines in stayover travel
in 2018. The findings come in the lead up to
the 500th anniversary of Havana as the
destination prepares for the expansion of its
cultural programming and infrastructure.
